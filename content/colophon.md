I bought [hiran.in](https://hiran.in) in 2007, when blogs were shaping the early web. It began as a redirect to Blogspot, moved through WordPress, and over time became home to experiments, staging sites, prototypes, and even a [DokuWiki](https://www.dokuwiki.org/dokuwiki), where I listed my books and movies for friends to borrow!

Over the years, it became an archive, a playground, and a place to try new ideas. [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com) brought digital gardening and led to building [Jekyll Garden](https://github.com/hfactor/jekyll-garden). Today, it lives here as a [Hugo](https://gohugo.io) as a commonplace for projects, writing, experiments, and things worth keeping.
