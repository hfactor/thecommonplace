---
title: Generalists have advantages
date: 2026-06-16
tags: [learning, thinking]
---

Specialisation is not always an advantage. Having exposure across domains helps you solve problems from different angles. An analogy from an adjacent business often articulates a problem more clearly than staying inside the domain. It also helps find edge cases faster, someone who's seen the same shape of problem elsewhere already knows where it breaks.

[[Look for rabbit holes]] is how this exposure compounds, following tangents across domains is what builds the cross-domain pattern recognition.

AI is making this more concrete. It raises the floor across disciplines to roughly average, which means someone with senior judgment in one area can now cover the adjacent ones well enough to complete a full project without a team. The generalist advantage isn't just about thinking differently anymore. It's about being able to act end-to-end.
