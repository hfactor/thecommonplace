---
title: Notes grow in value when written to evolve
date: 2021-06-09
---

A note written to be finished is less useful than one written to be returned to. Treating a note as a record stops the work at capture, and the archive becomes static. The compounding happens in revision: when you come back six months later and the note surprises you, disagrees with you, or needs updating, that friction is the system working. [[Evergreen notes should be atomic]] is what makes revision possible because a narrow note can be meaningfully updated rather than expanded until it becomes vague.

A note that never changes probably is not being used. Building the habit of returning matters as much as the habit of capturing. [[Better thinking over better note-taking]] only becomes real when the notes are being thought with rather than filed away. The useful note is the one that can interrupt you later.
