---
title: Documenting meetings
date: 2021-06-25
---

There are two kinds of notes from a meeting: the formal minutes and the ones you keep for yourself. The personal ones are more useful because they capture decisions, questions, and follow-ups rather than a transcript. Writing down the shared assumptions that did not get said out loud is part of this, and [[Explaining the obvious]] in a note costs nothing while silence about context creates gaps that surface as confusion months later. A daily note works better than one note per meeting: every meeting becomes a heading, and standard tags like Summary, Decision, Question, and Todo make search possible.

The point is not to preserve the meeting. It is to preserve what someone will need later. [[Design Decision Log]] captures the bigger calls that need more than a bullet point. A meeting without a clear purpose produces notes that are equally unclear.
