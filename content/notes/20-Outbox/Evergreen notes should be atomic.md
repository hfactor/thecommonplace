---
title: Evergreen notes should be atomic
date: 2021-06-13
---

One concept per note, not one topic. A topic like design systems can hold infinite content, but [[Design debt as a performance indicator]] is finite and arguable. Atomic notes are easier to reuse because their scope is clear: you know when they apply and when they do not. That precision is what makes [[Note titles are APIs for thought]] work in practice rather than just in principle.

Notes that try to hold too much become landfill. Everything goes in, nothing comes back out. [[Notes grow in value when written to evolve]] only happens when the note is narrow enough to be meaningfully revised rather than perpetually expanded. A broad note feels complete because it is full, not because it is useful.
