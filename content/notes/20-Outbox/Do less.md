title: Do less
date: 2021-07-03
---

Doing less is not the same as doing nothing. It's prioritising ruthlessly enough that the most important work gets real attention. Every time you add something to the list, something else gets less. The more useful question isn't what should I add, but what can I stop. [[LNO Framework]] makes the overhead category visible so that question has a data-backed answer rather than an instinct-based one.

The wall-staring time, genuinely unstructured and device-free, is part of doing less. [[Disconnect to connect]] depends on exactly this kind of intentional emptiness. [[Third dimension to Eisenhower Matrix]] surfaces the same logic before the calendar fills: the leverage tasks are often the ones that look optional until you do the math on what they prevent.
