---
title: You don't need all devices to make good coffee
date: 2026-06-16
tags: [hobbies, tools]
---

Coffee gets made sophisticated quickly. The base minimum looks like a lot of equipment. But you don't need it, pick one method, start there. As you learn and experiment, you'll know what to add. More devices don't make you a better brewer. They just make the collection bigger.

This applies to every hobby with a gear problem: photography, analog note taking, bullet journaling, electronics. The initial kit list is always longer than it needs to be. Don't buy cheap items you'll need to replace, but keep it to what you actually need. Don't fall for FOMO on gear. [[Convenience has tradeoffs]], more equipment often means more to manage, not more capability.

Coffee tasting works the same way. After-taste notes are personal, they depend on what you know and what flavours you remember. Fruit flavours in coffee change geographically. They're indicators, not standards. Compare instead: this coffee is like that one, but sweeter. That's more useful than chasing a tasting note you can't identify.
